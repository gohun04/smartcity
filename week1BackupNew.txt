					<h1>
					<a name="_Toc428532323">
					Key players
					</a>
					</h1>
					<p>
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:107%;font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Making large video games is a team effort and it's important to know the key people who you will be working with, especially as you may be spending long hours trying to hit a deadline. In a gaming studio you will often be making assets that will be used by other team members. Some key people are...
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					3D Artist
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					The 3D artist is responsible for creating 3d assets for the game. A 3D artist has a general knowledge in creating 3D models and may have other roles such as texturing in a small studio. They will need skills in topology, polygon efficiency and general design principles (that you will learn about later). &nbsp;3D artists can become a specialist in modelling certain things that require different types of skills. For instance a 3-D artist who creates models for characters will need to know anatomy and how the body needs to be shaped for animation, where a modeller for creating buildings will need to know about hard surface modelling. Some common areas of expertise for 3-D artists are characters, vehicles, buildings, weapons and environments. 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Software used:
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Maya, 3DS Max, Mudbox, Z Brush, Cinemea 4D, Blender
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<i>3D model created by Scott Eaton </i>
					<a href="http://www.scott-eaton.com/">
					<i>www.scott-eaton.com</i>
					</a>
					<i>
					</i>
					</p>
					<p>&nbsp;</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">2D Artist</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">The 2D artist works on any 2D assets or needs for the game such as concept art, sprites and user interface. In some studios the 2D artist may also work on the textures. 2D artists 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Software used:&nbsp; 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Photoshop, Gimp, Illustrator, Flash
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">

					</a>
					<i>
					</i>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					&nbsp;
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">Character Artist</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					The character artist creates the characters used in games. They can use a variety of techniques including digital sculpting and box modelling. Character artists need the same skills as a 3D artist plus knowledge in anatomy and movement. Character artists should also have a good understanding of design principles such as silhouette, form and function. 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Software used:
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Maya, 3DS Max, Mudbox, Z Brush, Cinemea 4D, Blender
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<i>Character art created by Gavin Goulder </i>
					<a href="http://www.gavimage.com/">

					</i>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">&nbsp;</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">&nbsp;</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">Texture Artist</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					The texture artist provides the material and colours that are placed on top of the models. They use a variety of tools such as Photoshop to create 2D textures, and substance painter to paint directly onto the model. Texture artist may work with a large range of textures from plants and trees to skin and fur. </span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">

					Maya, 3DS Max, Mudbox, Z Brush, Cinemea 4D, Blender, Photoshop, Substance Painter
					</span>
					</p>

					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">&nbsp;</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">Animator</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Animators take the 3D models and make them move. There may be 2-D animators in the gaming filed, but most will be 3-D. Animators can become specialist in fields such as character animation, special effects animation, vehicles and more. They should have knowledge of anatomy and movement as well as the principles of animation to add realism to the movements of objects within the game. 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Software used:
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">

					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">Programmer</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Programmers add the interaction to the game by creating the code or codebase.&nbsp; It is the programmer's job to implement assets, mechanics and physics into the game. Programmers may work generally on the game more specifically on areas such as the game engine, AI, sound, physics, graphics, gameplay and more. 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Software used:
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Unity, Cry Engine, Unreal Engine, Microsoft Visual Studio, Eclipse, X Code
					</span>
					</p>

					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">

					<i>
					</i>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">&nbsp;</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">Level Designer</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					A level designer makes sure the levels are designed and laid out correctly. The level designer may also work on encounters and missions within the game. They use a variety of tools, but will most likely use an in house level editor.
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Software used:
					</span>
					</p>

					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">

					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<i>Mission design for Assasin's Creed 2 by Francois Roughol&nbsp; </i>
					<a href="http://www.froughol.com/">

					</a>
					<i></i>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">&nbsp;</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">Game designer</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt"><span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					The game designer designs how the game will work. They come up with the rules that run the gameplay and have skills and understanding in many areas of the game development process. It's up to the game designer to define the core elements of the game. The game design document that they create will be used by all members of the development team. 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">

					Sound engineers look after all the sound that will be in the game including effects, voice overs and music. Sound engineers should be able to compose and perform music and use technology and software to get the best sounds and music into the game. 
					</span>

The role of game tester is often misinterpreted as a role where you do nothing but play games all day. While the tester will be playing the game, they must be playing with a critical mind looking for bugs and trying to break the game. The tester may have to play the same level many, many times.

<div class="GCLearningBody">
					<a name="_Toc428532324">
					<span class="Heading1Char">
					<span style="font-size:16.0pt;line-height:107%">Pipeline</span>
					</span>
					</a>
					<p>
					</p>
					<p>
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:107%;font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					The production pipeline is the process assets go through from concept to completion. The pipeline will be different for each studio, but no matter where you are the process should be simular. Watch the video to learn about a typical 3d production pipeline. 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p>
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:107%;font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Stages of a typical game production pipeline:
					</span>
					</p>
					<p>
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					The Concept: The basic idea of the game is conceived. This idea could come from anywhere and be based on anything. If the idea is considered good then the next phase begins. 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<b>
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Pre- Production
					</span>
					</b>
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:107%; font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					: 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					The pre- production phase sees the writers, designers, artists and programmers get together to create the game design document, story, storyboards and concept art. At this stage a prototype may be made of the game.
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<i>
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Game storyboards copyright blizzard entertainment
					</span>
					</i>
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<b>
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Production
					</span>
					</b>
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					:
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					With the game design document completed, or nearly completed the game moves into the production phase. Here we see more artist, designers and programmers brought in. The artists will now be creating models and animations. The programmers will be creating game systems and the designers will be constantly looking at the game design document and making any changes as soon as possible. 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<i>
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Production modelling copyright blizzard entertainment
					</span>
					</i>
					</p>

					<span style="font-size:16.0pt;line-height:107%">
					Scheduling and Customer expectations
					</span>
					</span>
					</a>
					</p>
					<p>
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:107%;font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Keeping to a schedule is important in the digital media industry. Projects that don't stick to the schedule can be expensive, as more money will need to be pumped into a project that runs over time. Video games will often have a release date put in place many months before the game is finished. If the game isn't finished by the allocated date you will be letting down your customers from the people waiting to play your game to the producers and others involved in the games development. 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p>
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:107%;font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					A common scheduling practice within the video game development industry is to use a Gantte Chart. Gantt charts represent the project schedule in a bar chart. The bar shows the start and end date of each production piece. 
					</span>
					</p>

					</p>

					Communication
					</span>
					</span>
					</a>
					</p>
					<p>
					</p>
					<p>
					<span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 107%; font-family: 'Times New Roman', serif;">
					Good communication skills are important. While working on video games you will need to share information and ideas with your other team members. To make sure the project is completed on time and follows the design good communication is needed
					</span>
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					and you will be doing that through several communication methods.
					</span>
					</p>
					<p></p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					Verbal Communication
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Verbal communication uses speech to communicate. It requires the use of tone and stress cues to portray what the speaker is communicating. 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					Non-verbal Communication
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Non-verbal communication uses techniques such as body language, facial features and even the cloths you wear. 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					Written Communication
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Written communication uses words to communicate your message. This could be in a written letter, email or blog post. 
					</span>
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					</p>
					<p style="text-indent:36.0pt">
					Listening Skills 
					</p>
					<p style="margin-left:36.0pt">
					<span style="font-size:12.0pt; line-height:107%;font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif">
					Listening is an essential skill for communication. By practicing good listening skills you will better understand what is being communicated to you. You can improve your listening skills by looking at the person speaking to you, don't speak while they are speaking and by repeating and asking questions about what is being said. 
					</span>
					</p>
